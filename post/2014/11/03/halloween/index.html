I set the bar low on costumes this year, so next year will DEFINITELY be an improvement.

Posted on November 3, 2014 by Lisa

I’ll be honest, the costume part of Halloween almost didn’t happen for us this year.

The past few weeks have been crazy and we’ve been trying to get Clara into some sort of sleep schedule and even though we had a party we were looking forward to being at, we kind of completely forgot to plan costumes to wear to that party.

Whoops.

Don’t worry, we threw something together last minute.

Exhibit A:

My hair was made for the 80’s.

Of course, Clara had her costume/outfit ready, because I was not about to lose the chance to take her picture as a pumpkin!

(Also, I’ve been putting her in her costume every-single-day this week, because babies can celebrate holidays (in which costumes are involved) longer than the rest of us can.)

If I’m being honest, I’ll just come out and tell you that Halloween has never been my favorite holiday to celebrate. Actually, I’ve never really been a ‘let’s go all out to celebrate this holiday’ type of person. I think I’ve always played the ‘oh gosh, life is too busy and this holiday snuck up on me card.’ Also, I dislike the commercialization of holidays, but that’s kind of a different story. However, I’m happy to report that my lack of enthusiasm for holidays is changing because holidays are so much more fun with a baby/child!

I look forward to dressing her up in cute outfits. I’m excited to get some decorations for the Thanksgiving/Christmas seasons that we can put up every year so she can anticipate the holiday season. I’m psyched to start family traditions. I’m relieved that celebrations can be less about the bar scene and more about gatherings with close friends.

Honestly, (for me) it’s so much more fun being cheesy with a tiny person around to be cheesy with – and holidays are best when they’re cheesy. It makes everything a bit more special. Plus, it gives us extra inspiration to get out of the house to do fun things like go to a pumpkin patch, look at the leaves falling, take walks around the neighborhood, etc. The only thing we didn’t do this year was pumpkin carving, but that should be a fun activity for us next year!

We could be using Clara as an excuse to stay in (which would be quite valid, because being a mom/dad is tiring, goshdarnit!), but I choose to use her an excuse to get out and celebrate a bit more than we would otherwise. 

Cheers!
