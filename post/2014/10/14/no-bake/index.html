Bite-sized-no-bake-deliciousness.

Posted on October 14, 2014 by Lisa

You know, I was thinking the other day that I don’t really write a lot about the things in my life that I absolutely love and think you should love, too. I think it’s because I assume that no one cares what I like, but I get so many ideas from blogs that I’m going to attempt to be more active in posting about some of my favorite things in hopes that you’ll find (and love) something new. 

You’re welcome in advance.

Let me set the scene. Today has been another gloriously rainy day in Chicago, and Clara is starting to get the idea about what should be done during rainy days, because she napped alllll day. It was wonderful and it allowed me to indulge in one of my favorite activities: eating. But I didn’t just eat anything, I ate no-bake energy balls, which I made from scratch – because I am so domestic like that (and this recipe involves like 5 ingredients and no baking or cooking).

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1 cup sweetened coconut flakes
  • 1/2 cup ground flaxseed
  • 1/2 cup mini chocolate chips
  • 1 tsp vanilla

Directions:

  1. Mix ingredients
  2. Chill
  3. Roll into balls
  4. ENJOY

I crossed out two of those steps because if we’re being honest, I really just ate the mixture with a spoon as soon as I made it and there was no chilling or balling, but ideally, there would have been.

This is also why you don’t get a picture of the finished product, but I’m sure you get the general idea.

Ta-da! A delicious (and possibly healthy) snack for you to enjoy.

 Cheers!
