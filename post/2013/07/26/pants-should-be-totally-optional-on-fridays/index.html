Pants should totally be optional on Fridays.

Posted on July 26, 2013 by Lisa

(This title has almost nothing to do with this post… but don’t you agree that it would be awesome to have a ‘pants optional’ day?!)

Friday, Friday, Friday!!! I feel like this was one of those weeks where a ton of things happened. Not in my life personally, but in the world. I know this because Twitter tells me so. Anyway, I don’t really have much to say that I haven’t already – so let’s get onto my Friday Fours!

  • The CrossFit Games (you can watch them this weekend on ESPN3 or on their website)
  • Chicago’s weather. It’s been in the 70s all week and I cannot get enough of it.

4 Confessions:

  • On Thursday I didn’t put on pants until 8pm. I went straight from pajamas to bikini and hung out in that until dinner! Have I mentioned I love summer?
  • I may spend the rest of forever watching all of the episodes of America’s Next Top Model I can on Hulu.com. I just found this site and am obsessed.
  • I haven’t responded to blog comments all week. This is a major ‘oops’ for me. I honestly don’t know where my time went all week! But I absolutely love comments and I promise to get caught up. Phew, glad I got that off my chest.
  • I think that the first pic of Royal Baby George and his parents is adorable and even though I’m not really interested in the Royal Family in general, I just think William and Kate are the cutest couple.

Featured Blog:

I just love helping and supporting new bloggers, so of course I was thrilled to ‘meet’ Hannah from Words as Palindromes! If you don’t know what a palindrome is, you aren’t the only one (because I didn’t either)!. Luckily, she explains that in her About Me page. (Seriously, that’s where I learned what the heck it was. It’s clearly been too long since I’ve been in school.)

Hannah and I have some things in common: we both recently had our blogs redesigned, we both like to talk, we minored in Spanish, we love to travel, and we both spent 6 weeks abroad during a summer in college. The difference is, Hannah is still actually in college and that makes me feel super-old. Wah, wah.

Hannah is just the sweetest blogger and her posts have inspired me to be more thankful for the little things and to actually keep track of a list of life goals. (I swear, I wrote them once but totally lost the whole darn list. Fail.) She also writes great personal/introspective posts, which I absolutely love. She even finds time to cook in college! This is something I can’t relate to at all, but I totally admire. When I was in college, I managed to ruin Easy Mac. 

Anyway, don’t take my word for it (even though I’m a totally reliable source), go over and say ‘hello’!
